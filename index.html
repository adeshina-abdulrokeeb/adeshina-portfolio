About

I’m Adeshina Abdulrokeeb Eniola. I design prompt systems that bridge human intent and machine reasoning — not tricks, but structured thinking. I teach models to follow a clear mental model so teams can rely on AI outputs in production.

I studied Generative AI at DSN 3MTT Nigeria, where I focused on applied prompting, safety-minded design, and production-ready prompt flows. Since then I’ve built prompt frameworks for tutoring, content automation, and persona-driven assistants used in prototypes and early products.

My approach is practical: reduce ambiguity, encode assumptions, and test outputs against measurable success criteria. That’s how prompt engineering becomes a predictable product skill — not a guessing game.

What I do

Prompt Architecture

Design reusable templates & system prompts so teams get consistent, reliable answers — across channels and models.

Reasoning & Validation

Layered prompts and verification steps that help models explain, justify and check their answers — reducing hallucination risk.

Content & Automation

Scalable flows for producing, editing and tailoring content—optimised for clarity, tone and outcome.

Tooling & Integration

Selected projects

Code Tutor — adaptive learning prompts

Problem: learners get stuck on small misunderstandings that block progress.
What I built: an adaptive prompt system that diagnoses common JS errors, returns bite-sized hints, and escalates explanations only as needed.
Outcome: more efficient learning sessions and faster problem resolution in prototype tests.

LinkedIn Transformer — thread generator

Problem: long-form content rarely converts to short, engaging posts.
What I built: a pipeline of prompts that extracts key ideas, crafts hooks, and iterates style until a multi-tweet/thread-ready output is produced.
Outcome: produced posts with clearer hooks and stronger engagement in trial runs.

Persona Framework — branded assistants

Problem: inconsistent tone across automated responses undermines brand trust.
What I built: constrained persona specs + memory patterns that keep brand voice steady across sessions.
Outcome: prototypes that felt noticeably more human and dependable during user tests.
